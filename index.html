Why Kuwait Uses So Much Plastic

1 · Culture of Convenience

Supermarkets still give double plastic bags, and takeaway meals arrive in foam boxes and PET cups.

2 · Cheap Oil, Cheap Resin

Kuwait’s subsidised energy keeps virgin plastic prices low, undercutting recycled options.

3 · Recycling Gaps

Only ≈15 % of plastic is recycled nationwide.[2] Collection points are few and mostly private.

4 · Imports from the Region

The GCC is one of the world’s largest plastic-pellet exporters, and surplus stock leaks into local markets.[3]

Environmental, Health & Economic Impacts

Marine Life

Turtles in Kuwait Bay often mistake floating bags for jellyfish.[4]

Human Health

WHO warns micro- and nano-plastics have unknown long-term effects on lungs and gut.[5]

Economy

Beach clean-ups and tourism losses cost Gulf countries millions each year.[6]
